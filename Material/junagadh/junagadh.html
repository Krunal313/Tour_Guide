ABOUT JUNAGADH

Junagadh holds of the pride of being one among the oldest cities of Gujarat. It's history is as old as the times of Lord Krishna.

Junagadh was ruled by various rules. In A.D. 640, Chinese traveler Huen Sang visited Junagadh. After 1472 Mohmad Begda, Khalil Khan, Muzaffar, Sikandar, Bahadursha and Ibadatkhan were ruling Junagadh between 1573 to 1748 Mughals ruled over. Thereafter various Babis/Nawabs were ruling Junagadh upto 1947

Junagadh city is located at 21.52°N 70.47°E at the foot of Mount Girnar, with the Arabian sea to the southwest, Porbandar to the north, and Amreli to the east. It has an average elevation of 107 metres

Junagadh has a tropical wet and dry climate (Aw) bordering on a hot semi-arid climate (BSh), with two distinct seasons: a dry season from October to May, and a wet season from June to September.
